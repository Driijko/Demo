INFORMATION FOR ARTISTS

We are seeking submissions from Toronto and GTA artists who have been affected by the prison and judicial system, either personally through their own incarceration, or through the experience of a friend, family, or community member. All artistic practices will be considered for submission, including but not limited to digital media, sculpture, painting, spoken or written word, and others. Artworks will be selected by the curators and representatives from each of the projects' partners. Selected artists will have their original works in the exhibition and successful applicants will be paid an honorarium per applicable CARFAC fees, with the option to sell their work in the exhibited space to earn extra income through the project. Deadline for submissions is September 6th, 2021.

See project posterhere.

SUBMISSION GUIDELINES

To be considered for this project please email ccproject2022@gmail.com with the following:

  • An artist’s bio and/or an artist’s statement (Max. 500 words)
  • 5-10 samples of your work
  • Why you would like to be involved in this project.Please explain your exprience with incarceration and how your art connects. With consideration of sensitive or possibly triggering subject matter, you may disclose as much or as little as you feel comfortable doing. Filmed or written replies will be accepted (Max 5 minutes or 500 words).
  • CV (Optional)

Accepted formats: PDF, word, pages, mp3, mp4, Google Drive link

Please clearly name any submitted documents with your first and last name. Submissions are evaluated by the curators and project partners. We encourage you to self-identify in your submission. Submissions are open to artists of all skill levels, and applications from Black, Indigenous, Persons of Colour and/or LGBTQ2S+ will be prioritized.
